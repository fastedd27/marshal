# Marshal: Project Brief

**Client: me.** I run technical operations for a residential smart-home integration company in Central Florida. Every job ends the same way: a tech writes the site document and hands it to support. Offsite support works the first call; my advanced team takes the escalations.

**What's broken: I am the gate.** Six to ten closeouts route through me a month, and sixty percent come back needing real work: a camera system with no recorder credentials, a control system three rooms use but nobody documented, a remote-access path no one confirmed. Each costs an hour or two of chasing the tech and re-checking. Accountability that should sit with the tech lands on mine. If I fix every doc, I might as well fill them out myself.

**What I've tried:** stricter templates and a second-tech review pass. Neither produced a clean, service-ready report, because nothing reads the answers back and asks the obvious next question. And not every blank is a gap: some sites have no control, no lighting, no AV rooms. Telling a real gap from a system that was never there is the hard part.

**What I need: a reviewer that sits where I sit.** The tech submits a finished doc, and before it is accepted the reviewer decides per system whether support can service it, then routes the exact gaps back to the tech to fix. The rare call a tech cannot make goes to the production manager, not my desk.
